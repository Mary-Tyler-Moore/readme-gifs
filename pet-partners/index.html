Pet Partners

A Quasar SPA deployed to Netlify



Motivation:

A student of mine asked for help creating a Quasar project. This student uses Windows 10 so the screen recordings will reflect this fact. My preference is to develop on Mac but I am doing this is as a personal favor for my student. If you would like to see a guide for installing Quasar CLI on Mac please check here.


Project Background:

Pet Partners is a fictional Pet Rental Service created by my daughter. She is my #1 UX/UI tester and has extensive experience catching bugs in my cross-platform applications. She is the of my life!


What to Expect:

Lots of cute pictures of pets. We are starting off by offering cats dogs and rabbits on short-term lease, but we are also open to exotic varieties of pets as well. Pet Partners will house and nurture any type of pet as long as that pet brings their owner joy. Our mission is to bring joy to everyone through the power of pets. If your pet brings you joy there is an high probability that your pet will bring someone else joy as well.

Please make a Pull Request if you would like to add your favorite pet to the list below.

Pet Rental List:

  • Dogs
  • Cats
  • Rabbits
  • Baby Yoda


Target Market:

Pet Partners will aggressively market to anyone having a bad day . In case of an emergency, our clients can contact us on the hotline listed on our site. In the coming weeks we will be adding a Pet Booking and Reversavation system as well.

The mission of Pet Partners is to brighten people's day with our loving, and well behaved pets. We at Pet Partners are committed to offering pet accessibility to those who are sick, depressed or stressed out!


Step 1 - Clone this Repository

Clone this Repository

git clone https://github.com/Mary-Tyler-Moore/pet-partners.git

cd pet-partners


Step 2 - Install and Build

Install project dependencies and build for production.

npm install

quasar dev

quasar build -m spa


Step 3 - Push :raisedhands: Build Folder **_dist/spa**

Open VS Code. Navigate to Source Control. Give the commit a memorable message, something that will clearly communicate to your coworkers the exact purpose of this commit.

For this example I gracefully wrote the message: "baby-yoday" .



A warning message pops up. You will often see this warning message when working on a project with multiple people making commits to the same branch. Notice how we are not able to push our code until we first pull the updated code from GitHub.


Step 4 - Deploy to Netlify
